they tell me love comes when i least expect it

jan 14 2025

they tell me that love comes when i least expect it. fuck that. love will come when it’s meant to.

that’s why i’ve stopped expecting it at all. that’s why i bury myself in textbooks, poring over every microscopic detail of the human heart...

that’s why i pile on back-to-back hospital shifts, running from one patient’s room to the next, letting the constant, frantic urgency of saving someone else’s life silence the screaming void inside my own.

but sometimes, shit sometimes, i stop. just for a second. on the drive home, when the city is too quiet and the moon hangs heavy and low...

and then people—oh, the audacity of people—have the nerve to say, “don’t worry, love will come at the right time.”

i didn’t come into this world waiting. i came out of the womb prematurely, lungs screaming like i already knew the world wasn’t ready for me...

let me raise a fat middle finger to the universe and keep grinding.

because i’m single. i’ve been single for most of my life...

until it wasn’t. until the thrill started wearing off...

afterward, i rip myself to fucking shreds...

am i not enough? am i too much?

i just want someone to tell me that they see me. not just the pieces i choose to show...

and honestly, why the fuck do heads fit so perfectly in the nooks of shoulders?

my friends. the ones with their perfect little relationships...

people say you’re a mosaic of the people you’ve loved before, and they’re not wrong...

so why do i still feel so fucking empty?

and so i keep running. i keep listening to the same goddamn sad love songs on repeat...

love doesn’t get to show up on its own terms, waltzing into my life when it feels like it...

so, no. i don’t sit around waiting for “the right time.” i bury myself in my work...

but i’m not okay. and i know it.
